How can you pursue your wants, if you can’t access your needs?

Help us make a differece

Problem

87% of Indian retailers are male owned; as a result, women’s needs are overlooked.
The problem isn’t product scarcity - it’s access.


Poverty used to be a reflection of scarcity. Now it is a problem of identification, targeting and distribution.
That is a problem that can be solved.

Solution

Azadi listens. Through integrating women into the product design and distribution mechanisms; Azadi provides the road for women to walk towards equality.


Azadi is not for women; it is by them. By integrating the end-consumer within the product design and distribution mechanisms, Azadi establishes a network where the rural Indian woman is important.


Providing > Profiting

Azadi’s products aren’t merely demanded by women; they’re designed by them. By undertaking a due diligence process to understand the affordability, design, and quality parameters specified by our end-consumers, our products are optimized to the rural demographic’s wants.


Making Relationships into Networks

Our philosophy is not to build a home when we can merely open a door. By identifying female social influencers, Azadi distributes its products through a female entrepreneurial network - maintaining a trusted channel for women.


Turning Potential Into Progress

Through providing high-impact lifestyle products and engineering an entrepreneurial network, Azadi lays the foundation for women to unleash their potential. By eliminating the barrier to access, we open the doors to aspiration.
