Happy [almost] or [belated] New Year, you plant-party person, you! ♥
Talia Pallock on December 21, 2014

Ahh, the new year. A time of reflection, celebration, and a massive hangover. I'm not sure what I love more about New Year's Eve - the confetti and party hats, or looking back of the year to remind myself of all that's happened.

When you take a look at all you've accomplished in 2014, think about those little things you did that helped you accomplish the big stuff.

Thank yourself for doing those tiny little things that at the time didn't seem like much (or seemed super annoying). See how they've contributed to where you are today. And then put your head down and get going on another year filled with little stuff like eating more kale, trying lentils and making banana ice cream instead of buying Ben & Jerry's.

While your'e at it, I've put a list together of the Top 14 Healthy Eating Things I Learned in 2014.

14 things that, individually, seem kinda mundane but when stacked up and applied day after day, have lead to me being profoundly healthier and happier than I was exactly a year ago.

Below is a sneak peak of my favorite three.

Let me know what you think! What takeaways did you have in 2014?

Dropping a label is ok. (Hard. Weird. Scary. But ok.)

In early 2014, I dropped my “vegan” label, one which I’d held for over 4 years, to go start dabbling with fish and eggs. It was hard letting go of something that I had clutched onto for comfort, rigidity and identity. It was weird since I’d probably said the sentence, “I can’t eat that, I’m a vegan” every day for 4 years straight. (Sometimes even twice a day!) It was scary because I had used that label to define me and my every bite of food, so being without it seemed as drastic as if I just up and decided to abandon my iPhone. But it was also the best thing I’ve ever done, because I feel so much better doing the #2 thing I learned…

Listening to your body is a must.

No truer words spoken. [Written.] (Except for every single word in every single John Mayer song, am I right?)

In 2014 I learned that I’d rather listen to my body than listen to cookbooks. This was huge! As someone who’d spent years and years following other peoples’ guidelines for what and what not eat, breaking free of that was life-changing. And I can honestly say I feel better than EVER, as I am:

a.) eating what my body’s asking for WHEN it’a asking for it – and no, Skittles and buttery popcorn at the movies doesn’t happen…even if my nose “asks” for it!

b.) I’m not so tied to my former food rules, which means less stress, which means less bellyaches and anxiety… which means I feel great so much more than ever before.

Protein powder is a lifesaver.

No truer words spoken. [Written.] (Before 2014, I was never a protein powder aficionado. But practically simultaneously I dramatically upped my fitness regimen and started to realize just how many carbs I was eating on a daily basis. To accommodate the butt-load of butt-toning squats I was doing daily, as well as to try to cut back on pastas, pastas and more pastas, I made protein a major priority. But I found getting a LOT of it to be tough - there’s just so much soy and beans a girl can toot…I mean take. :-P

So, my solution? PROTEIN POWDERS. PLANT-BASED PROTEIN POWDERS. (Not whey…no way.) Like Vega, Sunwarrior, Garden of Life, Sunfood, Shakeology, Perfect Fit….alll made from a combination of rice, hemp and other seeds, sweetened with stevia and containing at least 15 grams of protein per serving.

I make 1-2 protein and plant (greens and fruit) filled smoothies each day (like this heavenly one or this spoony one), I’ve started baking with protein powders in place of protein-void flours (like this carrot cake protein muffin) and I can hardly go a day without my beloved protein pancakes (or, on a special occasion – these BEST buckwheat protein pancakes).

I feel stronger, healthier, I have more muscle definition, I’ve lost fat and I eat much less carbs because I’m getting more filled up with protein and am less ravenous on a regular basis. Yay!
