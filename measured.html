Resposive Email Design

Mobile email readership continues to increase, surpassing desktop and webmail readership. With technology continuing to evolve, data is more readily available and easily accessible with mobile devices becoming many user’s first choice of device.

With more than half of opens now happen on mobile devices, responsive email design is a priority to continue innovating in the email marketing space. By applying Atomic Design principles to email, we increase click-through rates (CTR) and engagement with prospect advertisements.

Due to the fact that mobile usage has become the norm, user’s simply expect their applications and data to work properly on their devices. 70% of consumers delete emails immediately that don’t render well on a mobile device.


Responsive Email Templates



I customized and developed these email templates to show how responsive email can benefit advertisers. By using media queries and a few hacks, we are able to optimize email creatives for mobile.

Why Resposive Email Design?

  • Adds more control via CSS media queries.
  • Changes the layout of emails, adjusts the size of text, images, and buttons, and, in some cases, hides or even swaps content between desktop and mobile devices.
  • A complex, multi-column layout on desktop can be streamlined into a single-column, easy-to-scan, easy-to-scroll design on mobile—complete with larger text and more touch-friendly buttons.
  • Mobile-aware techniques act as a foundation for responsive emails and allows you to design an email that falls back gracefully in clients that don’t support media queries.
  • If a user at any point has trouble reading or clicking on an email then it's not working how it should be.



Email campaigns can have various effects on customer reach, both positive and negative. With a number of factors that come into play, it is important to consider things such as email design, messaging, opt-in/out options, and frequency. With all of the right factors, it’s been shown that email campaigns increase audience reach, click-throughs, and conversions.
